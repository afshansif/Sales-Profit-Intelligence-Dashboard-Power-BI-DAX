## 📌 Overview

A comprehensive Power BI dashboard built using advanced **DAX measures** to analyze sales performance, profitability, and growth trends across **2022–2024**. Designed to give business stakeholders a full 360° view of revenue health — from high-level KPIs down to month-by-month granular breakdowns.

## 📈 Visuals Included

### 1. Total Sales by Year and Month *(Line Chart)*
Tracks monthly sales fluctuations from 2022 to 2024, revealing seasonal patterns and performance trends over time.

### 2. Month-over-Month (MoM) Sales Growth *(Bar Chart)*
Highlights positive and negative monthly growth using color-coded bars — purple for growth, red for decline — making it easy to spot momentum shifts.

### 3. Total Profit by Customer Segment *(Bar Chart)*
Compares profitability across three segments:
- 🔵 Consumer
- 🏠 Home Office
- 🏢 Corporate

### 4. Total Profit by Product Category *(Pie Chart)*
Breaks down profit contribution by category:
- 🔵 Technology — 32.99% (1.03M)
- 🔷 Office Supplies — 32.11% (1.01M)
- 🟠 Furniture — 34.91% (1.09M)

### 5. Monthly Metrics Table
A detailed data table covering all key metrics per month including Sales YTD, Profit MTD, YoY Sales %, Sales Excluding Big Discounts, Total Profit, and MoM growth.

## 🛠️ Tools & Skills Used

- **Power BI Desktop** — dashboard design and layout
- **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** — custom calculated measures
  - `YTD` — Year-to-Date Sales
  - `MTD` — Month-to-Date Profit
  - `YoY %` — Year-over-Year Sales Growth
  - `MoM` — Month-over-Month Growth
  - `Sales Excluding Big Discounts` — filtered sales measure
- **Data Modeling** — date hierarchy, relationships
- **Conditional Formatting** — color-coded MoM bars

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- **Furniture** is the most profitable product category at **34.91%** of total profit
- **October–November** consistently shows the highest YoY growth spikes across all years
- **MoM growth is volatile** — frequent alternation between positive and negative months suggests seasonal buying patterns
- All three **customer segments perform similarly**, with no single segment dominating profit
- **Sales Excluding Big Discounts (7.90M)** vs **Total Sales (12.61M)** indicates heavy discount activity affecting ~37% of revenue
